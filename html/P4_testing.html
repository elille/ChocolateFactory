Testing Document

The questionnare can be found at: https://docs.google.com/forms/d/1rtp55keumMl0kzO4zvoHyLA6bJXISvF2921RnuFhuzw/edit

In brief, we asked how easy the site was to navigate on a scale of 1-5, if any problems were encountered while trying to navigate the page, if so; what the problem was. Then, the user is asked if the site is tidy or not. Now follows fact-questions in order to determine if the user has gotten the information that we want him/her to get.

Tester Audience

The users are friends of the developers, and are all 23-31 years old. It was simplest to ask our friends. There were three females and nine males among the testers. One big group the site aims at is kids, and they were not included in testing. This is since the site is for a thought-up client, and we thought that it was not important enough to ask kids.

Testing Platforms

7 persons used pc’s with windows with google chrome or mozilla firefox. 3 used macs with safari. The last two used iPads with safari.

Feedback Summary

The feedback was mostly positive:

Navigation:
On a scale of 1-5 where 5 is top, the users gave an average of 4.67.

16,7 %answered that they encountered a problem while navigating. One person reported: The main page was hard to find once it had been left. Another noted: Never mind, there was no problem.

Tidyness:
100% answered “yes” when asked if the site was tidy.

Content:
91,7 % answered correctly when asked when the factory was started. 33,3% answered correctly when asked who owns the factory. 10/12 and 11/12 were able to identify the products of the factory among other products, but 2/12 and 3/12 also added non-existing products to their forms, se result-chart below.

The results for the charities-content was: , where the correct answers were nr. 1, 3 and 4.

11/12 answered that the factory has a philanthropic engagement in the community. This was an important point to the customer, who wanted the users to notice this.

Commercialism:
100% answered that they now wanted something sweet.

Your Response

The most important feedback is that the site is easy to navigate and use. The fact that not all users are able to answer a quiz about the content proves that either the information is not well enough shown, or the users are not that interested in reading the content. Particularly some pages will not be read thoroughly, because the users are interested more in other parts. It is not surprising that the answers about charities is not as good as the one about the products. Products is also more important, since direct pr is one of the main aims of the site. Also, that the users reported that they now all wanted something sweet is very positive.

If it was wanted that the users remembered more facts, it would be a good idea to cut down on the text on the site. However, most users are not interested in learning and remembering facts, and so it is not considered very disappointing that they could not remember it.
