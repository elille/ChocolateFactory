Changes we have made

General

The font was changed to a nice webfont, bacause the Moon font was not availabel as a webfont. We fount another font that had the same "feeling" to it. We also made the whole page in Norwegian, so the sketches from the P2 are a bit dated.

Contest winner

Here we changed the design of the golden ticket. We also added a unique, and random generated code to the ticket, to make sure no one could cheat in our contest. Charities

In this page we changed the placing of the coin illustrations. This was just a pure design desition from our (and Wonkas) side. This was to make it just a little more distiguishable from the career page. In stead of the "read more" we put the headings of the sponsorship programs and other charities as collapsable links. This lets the user choose what information they want to see on the page. We also made the email adress clickable, and by clicking the adress the user will be prompted to send an email from their installed email client.

Jobs

Here we added a fun little spinning animation to the candies. This was done mainly to hypnotize people into applying for the poster jobs (but don't tell them that).

Like in the product suggestion page we had to removed the script that sends emails.

Modularity requirement

Regarding the modularity requirement, after the page was wireframed and its basic design set, the team took a step back and looked at the individual parts to see if any code was re-used in another area of the website, and took steps to move such snippets into locations where they could be referred to instead of re-defined in each page.

CSS commands that turned out to be useful in other sections was moved from the individual pages' CSS files, into the general "mainStyle.css"-file, so that the individual pages' CSS files only included tweaks and fixes for those particular pages. This ensures a more unified design, and simpler management and further development.

The Navigation bar was initially declared on every single page, which makes for very complicated management and lots of code repetition. These were replaced by a snippet of javascript that inserts the required elements straight into the pages.

In order to increase the legibility of the codes and also to allow possible future re-use of javascript functions, all snippets of javascript from the pages were moved into separate javascript files.
